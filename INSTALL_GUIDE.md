# How to Install Otto

Otto installs in under a minute. There's no software to download, no folders to drag, no settings to fiddle with. If you can install an app on your phone, you can install Otto.

---

## Before you start

You need:

- **Claude Desktop** installed on your Mac or PC. If you don't have it yet, download it free at [claude.ai/download](https://claude.ai/download).
- A **Claude account** (free or paid both work).

That's it.

---

## Install in 4 steps

### Step 1 — Open Cowork

Open Claude Desktop. In the top of the window, click the **Cowork** tab. Cowork is the side of Claude built for getting real work done — it's where Otto lives.

### Step 2 — Open the plugin browser

In the left sidebar of Cowork, click **Customize**. Then click **Browse plugins**. A panel will open showing the plugins you can install.

### Step 3 — Add the Otto marketplace

At the top of the plugin browser, paste in this address:

```
https://github.com/arslvn93/otto
```

Press enter. Otto will appear in the list.

### Step 4 — Click Install

Find Otto in the list and click the **Install** button next to it. That's it. Otto is now ready.

## First time using Otto

Open a new Cowork conversation and just say **"hi"** — or jump straight in with something like *"write me a just listed Instagram post."*

The very first time you talk to Otto, it will pause and say something like:

> *"Before I can help with that, I need about two minutes to get to know you."*

Otto will then ask you a few quick questions in plain English:

- Your name, brokerage, phone, and email
- What areas you work and what you specialize in
- Your social media handles (optional)
- How you want Otto to sound — Professional, Warm, Casual, Luxury, or "match my style"
- How you sign off your emails
- Anything personal you want Otto to weave into your content (your dog, the team you sponsor, the fact that you never use exclamation marks, etc.)

That whole conversation takes about two minutes. Once you're done, Otto saves your profile and creates an **Otto Workspace** folder for you with seven category folders inside (Listings, Buyers, Open Houses, Offers, Post-Close, Marketing, Prospecting). Every email, listing, post, and checklist Otto writes from now on gets saved into the right folder automatically.

You only do this once. Otto remembers everything forever after.

## Using Otto day-to-day

You don't need to call Otto by name. Just ask Claude in plain English for whatever you need:

- *"Write a listing description for 742 Maple Drive — 3 bed, 2 bath, 1,600 sqft, new kitchen."*
- *"Draft an open house follow-up email for Saturday's visitors at 310 Maple."*
- *"My buyer wants to wait for interest rates to drop. Give me talking points."*
- *"Roleplay a FSBO call with me."*
- *"Write a price reduction email — 45 days on market, recommending a $20K drop."*
- *"Just sold post for 88 Harbor View, $15K over asking, first-time buyer."*

Otto will pick the right template, write the content in your voice, and save it to the right folder. Every response ends with a "Saved to:" line so you know exactly where the file lives.

## Updating your profile

If anything about you or your business changes, just tell Otto in plain English:

- *"Update my profile — my new phone is (416) 555-0123."*
- *"I switched brokerages. I'm now at Compass."*
- *"Change my email sign-off to 'Talk soon, Jess.'"*

Otto will rewrite the relevant section and confirm it. No file editing needed.

## Troubleshooting

**"Otto isn't showing up after I clicked Install."**
Quit Claude Desktop completely (right-click the icon in your dock or system tray and choose Quit), then reopen it. Otto should appear.

**"Otto didn't ask me the onboarding questions."**
Make sure you're starting a brand new conversation, not continuing an old one. Onboarding only triggers on the very first message of your very first session.

**"Otto wrote something in the wrong tone."**
Tell it. Say *"that's too formal — make it warmer"* or *"shorter, more punchy."* Otto adjusts on the fly. If you want a permanent change, say *"update my profile — change my tone to Warm & Approachable."*

**"I want to start over with onboarding."**
Just say *"Otto, let's start over with onboarding"* and it will rerun the full setup conversation.
